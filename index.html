Executive Overview
Week 12 · 25 March 2026
Market Summary: The European dairy market enters Q2 2026 with a distinctly supply-heavy tone. EU raw milk deliveries rose +4.7% year-on-year in January — reaching a new historical record for full-year 2025 at 148.5 million tonnes. Processing capacities are running at high utilisation, pressing spot raw material prices lower. Against this supply backdrop, finished product markets are showing a notable divergence: Skim Milk Powder (SMP) is surging (+7.0% vs. 4-week average), driven by strong export demand — particularly from Mexico, which recorded SMP imports up +47% in 2025. Butter remains elevated near €4.37/kg, supported by seasonal Easter demand and tight fat supply. Sweet Whey Powder (SWP) has reached new all-time highs, while WPC 80 demand is described as exceptionally strong at premium prices. Geopolitical uncertainty surrounding the Iran conflict is pushing energy and fertiliser costs higher, adding a cost-side risk to the otherwise positive demand picture.

Supply & Milk Pool
Milk Supply Fundamentals: The EU-27 milk pool is expanding at a pace not seen in recent cycles. January 2026 deliveries hit 12.3 million tonnes, up 4.7% year-on-year. Full-year 2025 reached a new historical record of 148.5 million tonnes, with content deliveries (fat + protein) growing even faster at +2.5%, as average fat content rose to 4.29% and protein to 3.53%. The largest absolute gains came from Germany (+7.0%, +0.2 Mt), France (+5.8%), and the Netherlands (+5.8%). Belgium surged +10.9%.

EU & World Prices
Price Dynamics: EU commodity prices present a bifurcated picture heading into Q2. SMP has surged +7.0% vs. the 4-week average to €2.60/kg, making it the standout performer — driven by strong MENA and Latin American demand. Butter is firm at €4.37/kg with Easter pre-buying providing a near-term floor. WMP is recovering at €3.40/kg (+2.9%). Cheddar softened slightly (−1.2%), reflecting adequate supply. Sweet Whey Powder hit new historical highs while WPC 80 is commanding premium prices. At the GDT Event 400 (17 March), AMF rose +6.4%, SMP ticked +5.2%, Butter was unchanged, and WMP fell −4.0%.

South German Exchange Report — 25 March 2026
The Süddeutsche Butter- und Käse-Börse reports stable butter prices in pre-Easter trade with retail volumes above prior year levels. Block butter appears to have reached a preliminary price peak, though the forward outlook remains uncertain. Cut cheese demand is excellent for Easter with full order books. Export-oriented block goods are actively traded, and domestic sales are running well. Slotted cheese (Schnittkäse) spot prices for April could still move higher. May remains uncertain given tensions around Iran and US trade policy.
Whey & SWP Focus
Whey & Whey Proteins — Market Update: Sweet Whey Powder (SWP) is the standout commodity of the current cycle, having reached new all-time price highs. The gap between food-grade and feed-grade SWP is narrowing as both segments firm simultaneously. Most critically for BMi's product portfolio, WPC 80 is in exceptionally high demand and is achieving premium prices — a function of tightening athletic nutrition and infant formula demand globally combined with limited high-purity processing capacity across Europe. WPC 35 also benefits from the buoyant SWP floor. Production of whey products in the EU rose +1.8% YoY in January 2026, reflecting the broader milk supply expansion. China's whey powder imports rose +10% in Jan–Nov 2025, with EU's share of the Chinese market steady at approximately 33–35%.

Lactose Market: EU lactose exports fell −18% in Jan–Nov 2025 to 157,867 tonnes, a significant contraction. However, GDT Lactose held firm at $4,925/MT (+0.1%) in the latest auction. China's lactose imports from the EU grew +19% (164 kt), partially offsetting declines elsewhere. This suggests a geographic re-routing of lactose trade flows toward Asia. Demand from the pharmaceutical excipient sector provides a structural floor for quality lactose pricing.
Global Trade
EU Export Performance: EU dairy exports show a divergent picture by product. SMP export volumes rose +10% in Jan–Nov 2025 to 731,187 tonnes — the standout performer. Cheese exports grew +1% to 1.29 million tonnes, with the UK remaining the dominant destination. Butter exports were essentially flat (−0%). WMP fell −18%. The EU's SMP competitiveness is particularly strong in the current period, with European product considered highly competitive on world markets. Mexico is a key growth destination for SMP (+47% YoY). The UAE is emerging as a significant growth market across multiple categories.

⚠️ US Trade Policy Risk: The US tariff environment under the current administration represents a wildcard for global dairy trade flows. Any escalation could redirect US export volumes (particularly SMP and cheese) away from target markets, creating short-term disruption but potentially opening alternative demand for EU suppliers in displaced markets. The Iran conflict remains a persistent driver of MENA supply uncertainty, which structurally supports EU ingredient exports to the region.
Feed & Energy Markets
Cost Pressure Alert: Feed and energy costs are rising sharply, posing a meaningful margin risk for EU dairy farmers. Energy costs surged +24.8% versus the 4-week average in Week 12 — the most significant cost-side pressure reading in recent quarters. Feed costs rose +2.5%. The Iran conflict is the primary driver, elevating oil prices (Brent at $101/bbl as of 22 March 2026) and restricting fertiliser supply from Gulf-region producers. These cost pressures, if sustained, could moderate the current milk supply expansion from mid-2026.

FX Markets
EUR/USD — Key Consideration for Dairy Trade: The EUR/USD rate currently stands at 1.1561 (25 March 2026), having pulled back from the year-to-date peak of approximately 1.1868 reached in mid-February 2026. Importantly, the Euro's appreciation has been a 2025–2026 story: the EUR started 2025 near multi-year lows around 1.02–1.04, then surged dramatically — climbing nearly 15% — as the US Dollar weakened on the back of tariff uncertainty and macro concerns. This means that while the EUR is off its February highs, it remains significantly stronger than where EU dairy exporters were pricing 12 months ago. The structural impact on EU export competitiveness is therefore meaningful: EU butter at €4.37/kg now translates to approximately $5,050/t, well above the US equivalent of $4,173/t. SMP exports have proven more resilient to FX headwinds due to quality-driven and relationship-based demand from MENA and Latin America. The EUR/JPY cross at ~183 JPY may also weigh on EU exports to Japan.
