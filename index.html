European Dairies: August 2025 Analysis

Market Updates

Executive Summary & Key Highlights

The European dairy market in August 2025 continues to be influenced by seasonal factors, global trade dynamics, and specific product-category performance. Overall market conditions are generally stable, with some notable shifts.

Key Market Observations (Week 33 / 17-20.08.2025):

  • EU Milk Deliveries: The seasonal decline in milk supply has slowed slightly. EU milk collected for Jan-Jun 2025 is up 0.6% YoY.
  • Raw Milk Prices: The Weighted EU Average for raw milk in June 2025 was 52.9 €/100kg. Italian spot prices (Lodi) were 58.8 €/100kg as of August 17.
  • Product Category Performance: Butter prices are firm, with packed butter showing good demand. Powder markets are mixed; SMP stable, WMP slightly down. Cheese markets (Emmental, Schnittkäse) show good demand.
  • Input Costs: Feed costs are slightly down (-0.2%), while energy costs are more significantly down (-3.0%) compared to the average of the previous 4 weeks (Week 33).
  • FX Rates: The Euro has maintained relative stability against the USD (EUR/USD 1.1682 as of 20.08.25).

Supply & Demand Dynamics

The European dairy market's dynamics in August 2025 are shaped by a slowed seasonal decline in milk collection, global trade, and evolving demand patterns. Overall EU milk collected (Jan-Jun 2025) shows a slight increase of +0.6% compared to the same period in 2024.

Demand Trends

Market Implications:

The slowed seasonal decline and slight year-on-year increase in EU milk collection provide a robust supply foundation. Strong internal demand for butter and cheese, especially from holiday regions and food service, is supporting prices. However, the strong Euro continues to be a hurdle for exports to non-EU countries, leading to price discussions and an inconsistent market in this segment. The return of buyers post-holidays might stimulate milk powder markets.

Feed Market Impact on EU Dairy

Feed and energy costs remain crucial factors for dairy farm profitability. August data shows a general easing in these costs compared to earlier in the summer.

Key Input Cost Trends (Week 33 / 17.08.2025):

  • Feed costs: **-0.2%** (compared to average of previous 4 weeks).
  • Energy costs: **-3.0%** (compared to average of previous 4 weeks).
  • Europe Brent Spot Price (Oil): **67.4 USD/Barrel** (17.08.25).

Note: Chart data for feed and energy costs reflect week-on-week changes.

Commentary:

The slight decrease in feed costs and a more significant drop in energy costs offer relief to dairy farmers, potentially improving farm margins. This positive trend for input costs provides a more favorable environment heading into the autumn. The market for feed-grade skimmed milk powder has also calmed down compared to previous weeks.

Geopolitical & Macroeconomic Factors

The EU dairy market's competitiveness and stability are influenced by global trade dynamics, economic conditions, and currency movements.

FX Rates (EUR)

  • EUR/USD Exchange Rate: **1.1682** (Last Update: 20.08.25).
  • The strong Euro makes it challenging for European exporters to sell goods to third countries, leading to price discussions.

Trade & Demand

  • Overall market conditions are generally **quiet** due to the holiday period.
  • First buyers are returning from holidays, potentially leading to a slight revival in demand for skimmed milk powder.
  • No whole milk powder is expected from the Algeria tender out of the EU.

Global Outlook

  • **NZ milk production (Jun-May 2024/25):** **+2.3%** YoY.
  • **US milk production (Jan-May 2025):** **+0.3%** YoY.
  • **Australian milk production (Jul-May 2024/25):** **-0.4%** YoY.
  • Global dairy trade auction (19 Aug 2025): average price **-$3,676 USD/MT**, **-0.3%** from previous.

Summary & Outlook

EU dairy markets in August 2025 are characterized by a slowed seasonal decline in raw milk, firm butter prices, and mixed signals in the powder and cheese sectors. Reduced input costs offer support to farm profitability, but the strong Euro continues to challenge export competitiveness. The end of the holiday season is expected to bring a slight revival in market activity.

Key Factors to Monitor:

  • Evolution of milk supply as the seasonal decline slows and potential for a "second milk peak" from last year's Bluetongue impact.
  • The strength of the Euro and its influence on third-country exports and global competitiveness.
  • Demand recovery in milk powder markets as buyers return from holidays.
  • Price developments in block butter as buyers observe the market.
  • Ongoing trends in feed and energy costs.
