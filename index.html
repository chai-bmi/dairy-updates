European Dairies: June 2025 Analysis

Market Updates

Executive Summary & Key Highlights

The European dairy market in June 2025 continues to exhibit a delicate balance, with firm underlying demand for certain products countered by persistent supply constraints and fluctuating input costs. While overall sentiment remains cautiously optimistic, the market is navigating significant external pressures from geopolitical developments and evolving trade dynamics. Strategic foresight and adaptability are crucial for navigating this complex environment.

Key Market Observations:

  • **EU Milk Deliveries:** Early 2025 EU milk deliveries show a slight decline compared to 2024, estimated to be about 0.2% lower, with notable declines in France, Germany, Netherlands, and Italy.
  • **Raw Milk Prices:** The average EU raw milk price in May 2025 was €0.5334/kg, up 0.6% from April and 16% above May 2024. June 2025 prices are expected to remain stable.
  • **Product Category Divergence:** Butter prices remain strong. Skimmed Milk Powder and Whole Milk Powder face downward pressure. Cheddar Cheese prices have softened. Whey Powder is stable.
  • **Input Costs:** Feed and energy costs have eased compared to 2024, supporting farm margins.
  • **FX Rates:** The Euro has remained relatively stable against major currencies through June 2025, with minor fluctuations.

The European dairy market's dynamics in June 2025 are shaped by supply constraints and evolving demand patterns, influencing the strategic product mix.

Supply Trends

  • Declines in France, Germany, Netherlands, Italy, partially offset by growth in Poland and Ireland.
  • Driven by herd reductions, disease outbreaks, environmental regulations.

Market Implications:

The slight decline in EU milk deliveries indicates structural supply constraints. The strategic shift towards cheese production will reduce availability for powders and butter, influencing their respective price dynamics. Global demand, particularly from China, remains a key variable for export performance.

Organic Milk Market Snapshot

The organic milk sector maintains a significant premium, with stable demand particularly in Northern and Western Europe, despite representing a smaller portion of total EU deliveries.

Market Position

  • Organic production accounts for approximately **3.5%** of total EU milk deliveries.
  • Prices maintain a **25–35% premium** over conventional milk.

Demand & Outlook

  • Demand is **stable**, especially in Northern and Western Europe.
  • Input cost relief is supporting farm profitability in the organic sector too.

Feed Market Impact on EU Dairy

Lower feed and energy costs compared to 2024 are supporting farm margins, providing some relief despite persistent structural challenges.

Key Input Cost Trends:

  • Feed and energy costs have **eased** compared to 2024.
  • This easing is supporting farm profitability.
  • However, structural challenges like herd shrinkage and environmental regulations persist.

Note: Feed prices are illustrative estimations for June 2025.

Commentary:

The reduction in feed and energy costs is a positive factor for dairy farmers, as it directly improves their margins and incentivizes production. This relief is particularly important given the ongoing structural challenges within the sector, such as shrinking herds and the impact of environmental regulations. However, despite this cost relief, these deeper structural issues mean that the industry cannot rely solely on lower input prices for long-term growth.

Geopolitical & Macroeconomic Factors

The EU dairy market's competitiveness and stability are influenced by global trade dynamics, economic conditions, and currency movements.

FX Rates (EUR)

  • The Euro has remained relatively **stable** against major currencies through June 2025, with minor fluctuations.
  • A stronger euro could weigh on EU export competitiveness, while a weaker euro supports exports.

Trade Tensions

  • Risks from trade tensions and animal diseases remain.
  • These can impact future demand for EU exports.

Economic Outlook

  • Overall economic sentiment supports EU dairy markets.
  • However, demand uncertainty remains in key export markets, notably China.

Summary & Outlook

EU dairy markets in June 2025 show firm raw milk and butter prices amid supply constraints and strong demand. Powder markets face mild price corrections, reflecting global demand uncertainties. Input cost relief supports farm profitability, but structural challenges persist. The market outlook remains cautiously optimistic, with production and demand balancing amid evolving global trade dynamics.

Key Factors to Monitor:

  • Evolution of global dairy demand, particularly China's import appetite.
  • Impact of structural challenges like herd shrinkage and environmental regulations.
  • Global risks from trade tensions and animal diseases.
  • Continued monitoring of input costs (feed, energy).
  • Overall balance of production and demand.
