Executive Market Commentary

The EU dairy market is currently characterized by high milk intake volumes meeting robust demand for processed goods. While raw milk collection shows a year-on-year increase of approximately 1.8%, processing facilities across Europe are operating at maximum capacity. This high utilization has led to a stabilization of prices for finished goods, despite the abundance of raw material.

In the powder sector, Skim Milk Powder (SMP) is experiencing a clear strengthening trend. Manufacturers report a surge in inquiries, with many securing new contracts at higher price levels. Feed-grade powder is following this trajectory, driven by scarcity in available offers. For Whole Milk Powder (WMP), the market has become more uniform as the lowest price offers have largely been withdrawn.

The cheese sector remains a primary pillar of stability. Schnittkäse (Sliced Cheese) demand is described as "continuously high" across retail and export channels. Ripening cellars for varieties like Cheddar and Emmentaler are reported to be at critical lows, with many producers unable to fulfill additional orders beyond existing commitments.

Critical Indicators

  • A. Processing Limits: European processing capacities are fully utilized; surplus milk is increasingly directed toward commodity production.
  • B. Stock Depletion: High export volumes have kept cheese stocks low, providing a price floor despite seasonal milk peaks.
  • C. Butter Stability: Block butter is stabilizing following a period of volatility, supported by recovering cream prices.
