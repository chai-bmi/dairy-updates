European Dairies: May 2025 Analysis

Market Updates

Executive Summary & Key Highlights

The European dairy market in May 2025 is navigating a complex and dynamic landscape, characterized by stabilizing raw milk prices, diverging performance across various product categories, and persistent structural supply limitations. While some input costs have shown signs of easing, new geopolitical tariffs are poised to exert upward pressure on expenses, creating a delicately balanced and inherently high-cost operating environment for producers. This intricate interplay of factors necessitates a strategic and adaptable approach for all market participants.

Key Market Observations:

  • Raw milk prices have largely plateaued in early 2025, holding significant year-on-year gains (approx. 15-16% higher), suggesting a "new normal" of higher raw milk prices.
  • A notable split in product pricing persists: Butter prices remain firm due to tight supplies, while Skimmed Milk Powder (SMP) continues to face downward pressure.
  • EU milk production is projected to decline marginally in 2025 due to shrinking herds, stringent environmental regulations, and disease risks.
  • Despite some stabilization in feed prices, the overall cost environment remains significantly elevated. New EU tariffs on Russian/Belarusian agricultural products (from July 1, 2025) are expected to increase input costs.
  • The continued strength of the Euro against the US Dollar and new US tariffs on EU dairy imports create headwinds for export competitiveness.

EU Raw Milk Market Overview

The raw milk market in the European Union has reached a point of stabilization in early 2025, following a period of substantial price increases. This plateauing, however, occurs at a significantly higher price level than previous years, reflecting a new market equilibrium.

Raw milk prices in the EU have largely stabilized around EUR 0.53-0.54/kg. This stability follows significant cost absorption in 2024. The continued year-on-year increase of over 15% underscores that higher cost structures and supply tightness are now integrated into the market. While upward momentum has ceased, prices remain structurally elevated, suggesting a new, higher baseline rather than a return to historical lows. This aligns with the European Commission's outlook for structurally higher EU milk prices.

Key Dairy Product Prices

The EU dairy market exhibits a clear divergence in product pricing. Butter maintains strength due to tight supplies and strategic milk allocation towards cheese, while Skimmed Milk Powder (SMP) faces downward pressure. Cheddar cheese shows signs of stabilization after an earlier slump.

Butter prices remain buoyant due to tight supplies and robust demand for milk fat. Conversely, SMP continues its downward trend, reflecting an imbalance in milk solids supply and demand. Supply & Demand Dynamics

The European dairy market in 2025 is shaped by constrained supply due to structural limitations and evolving, somewhat uncertain, demand patterns both globally and domestically.

Supply Trends

  • EU milk production forecast to decline by -0.2% in 2025 (to 149.4 MMT).
  • Jan-Mar 2025 milk production down -1.8% YoY. Germany (-3.7%), France (-3.1%).
  • Drivers: Declining herds, tight margins, environmental regulations (Green Deal), disease.
  • Strategic Shift: Cheese production +0.6%, Butter -1%, SMP -4%, WMP -5%.

Demand Trends

  • Global demand remains "shaky"; China's import appetite is a key variable (+2% forecast after 3-year decline).
  • EU dairy exports grew +8% in value (Jan 2025), largely due to price gains.
  • EU fluid milk consumption continues long-term decline (-0.3% in 2025).
  • EU consumer confidence up +1.4 pp in May 2025, but still below long-term average.

Market Implications:

The marginal decline in EU milk production reflects deep structural challenges, implying a long-term recalibration of supply capacity. This could underpin higher domestic prices and influence global trade.

The processors' shift to prioritize cheese fundamentally alters other product availability, creating tightness in butter and potential oversupply in powders, leading to divergent prices and new trade opportunities.

Geopolitical & Macroeconomic Factors

The EU dairy market is influenced by trade relations, domestic economic conditions, and currency fluctuations.

Geopolitical Factors

  • US-EU Trade Tensions: US imposed 20% tariffs on EU dairy imports (eff. Apr 9, 2025), impacting premium products.
  • EU-UK Trade: New SPS agreement (May 21, 2025) aims to ease food/dairy border checks.
  • Russia/Belarus Tariffs: New EU tariffs on agri-products (feed, fertilizers) from July 2025.
  • Disease Risks: HPAI in US cattle, Bluetongue in Europe pose ongoing supply risks.
  • Global Conflicts: War in Ukraine continues to disrupt supply chains.

Macroeconomic Factors (EU)

  • Inflation (Apr 2025): Euro area annual rate stable at 2.2%. Food contributed +0.57pp. Overall 2025 forecast: 2.1%.
  • Economic Growth (2025): EU economy projected +1.1%.
  • Consumer Confidence (May 2025): Increased +1.4pp but remains markedly below long-term average.

FX Markets (EUR/USD)

  • May 23, 2025 Rate: EUR 1 = USD 1.1301.
  • May 2025 Forecast Range: 1.1300 - 1.1530.
  • Impact: Strong Euro + US tariffs make EU dairy effectively ~3% more expensive vs US products, reducing export competitiveness.

Outlook & Key Considerations

The European dairy market in May 2025 is characterized by a "fragile balance." Adaptability, rather than broad-based expansion, is key in this new era of constrained growth. The market is transitioning into a period where strategic adaptation and robust risk management will be paramount.

Key Factors to Monitor:

  • Global demand evolution (especially China's import appetite).
  • EU policy & regulatory landscape (CAP, Green Deal).
  • Trade policy developments (US tariffs, UK-EU SPS agreement).
  • Input cost volatility (impact of new EU tariffs on feed/fertilizers).
  • Disease outbreaks (HPAI, Bluetongue).
  • FX rate fluctuations (EUR/USD impact on export competitiveness).
