European Dairies: July 2025 Analysis

Market Updates

Executive Summary & Key Highlights

The European dairy market in July 2025 continues to navigate a complex landscape, characterized by seasonal production shifts, evolving demand, and persistent macroeconomic influences. While overall market sentiment remains stable, specific product categories show varied performance.

Key Market Observations:

  • EU Milk Deliveries: Cooler summer weather is slowing the typical decline in milk volumes. Jan-May 2025 EU milk collected is down 0.8% YoY.
  • Raw Milk Prices: The Weighted EU Average for raw milk was 53.1 €/100kg up to June 2025. Italian spot prices (Lodi) were 65.8 €/100kg as of July 20.
  • Product Category Performance: Butter prices remain strong. SMP and WMP show slight weekly declines but are up year-on-year. Cheese markets (Cheddar, Gouda, Edam, Emmental) show mixed signals with some weekly fluctuations.
  • Input Costs: Feed costs are slightly down, while energy costs are slightly up compared to the average of the previous 4 weeks (Week 29).
  • FX Rates: The Euro has maintained relative stability against major currencies through July 2025 (EUR/USD 1.1699 as of 23.07.25).

Supply & Demand Dynamics

The European dairy market's dynamics in July 2025 are shaped by seasonal shifts in milk collection, persistent supply constraints, and evolving demand patterns.

Supply Trends

  • Cooler summer weather is slowing the typical seasonal decline in milk volumes across Germany.
  • Potential for a "second milk peak" later in the year due to last year's Bluetongue disease outbreak shifting calving periods.
  • Overall EU milk collected Jan-May 2025 is -0.8% compared to Jan-May 2024.
  • EU production of Condensed Milk is down -11.6% (Jan-May 2025 vs 2024).

The chart above illustrates the production changes for key EU dairy products (Jan-May 2025 vs 2024).

Demand Trends

  • Global dairy import demand, particularly from China, shows cautious recovery (forecasted +2.5% for Jan-Mar 2025).
  • EU domestic consumption for butter and cream remains strong.
  • Demand for dairy ingredients in the food manufacturing sector is stable, with increased activity expected from confectionery due to lower cocoa prices.
  • High demand for Schnittkäse from Southern Europe due to holiday season.

Market Implications:

The slower decline in milk volumes, possibly leading to a second peak, could influence supply dynamics later in the year. Strong domestic demand for certain products like butter and cheese, coupled with cautious global recovery, will shape processor strategies. The increased activity from the confectionery industry due to lower cocoa prices could provide additional demand for milk powders.

Organic Milk Market Snapshot

The organic milk sector maintains its premium position with stable demand, particularly in Northern and Western Europe.

Market Position

  • Organic production accounts for approximately 3.5% of total EU milk deliveries.
  • Prices maintain a significant premium over conventional milk.

Demand & Outlook

  • Demand is stable, especially in Northern and Western Europe.
  • Input cost relief is supporting farm profitability in the organic sector.

Feed Market Impact on EU Dairy

Feed and energy costs remain crucial factors for dairy farm profitability. While generally stable, recent trends show mixed movements.

Key Input Cost Trends (Week 29 / 20.07.2025):

  • Feed costs: -0.9% (compared to average of previous 4 weeks).
  • Energy costs: +1.4% (compared to average of previous 4 weeks).
  • Europe Brent Spot Price (Oil): 71.0 USD/Barrel (20.07.25).

Note: Chart data for feed prices are illustrative estimates based on the provided percentage changes.

Commentary:

The slight decrease in feed costs offers some relief to dairy farmers, supporting margins. However, the increase in energy costs indicates ongoing volatility in agricultural inputs. The overall market conditions for feed are generally quiet due to the holiday season. The slight decrease in the USD exchange rate has not yet provided additional export impulses for third countries, which could impact demand for certain dairy products.

Geopolitical & Macroeconomic Factors

The EU dairy market's competitiveness and stability are influenced by global trade dynamics, economic conditions, and currency movements.

FX Rates (EUR)

  • EUR/USD Exchange Rate: 1.1699 (Last Update: 23.07.25).
  • The Euro has maintained relative stability against major currencies through July 2025.
  • A stronger euro could weigh on EU export competitiveness, while a weaker euro supports exports.

Trade & Customs

  • Chaotic customs disputes are causing some uncertainty in the market.
  • The slightly decreased USD exchange rate has not yet provided additional impulses for exports to third countries.

Economic Outlook

  • Overall market conditions are generally quiet due to the holiday period.
  • Demand uncertainty remains in key export markets, notably China, influenced by global economic growth.

Summary & Outlook

EU dairy markets in July 2025 are characterized by firm raw milk and butter prices, seasonal shifts in production, and ongoing adjustments in powder and cheese markets. Input cost relief continues to support farm profitability, but structural challenges and global trade dynamics necessitate cautious optimism.

Key Factors to Monitor:

  • Impact of cooler summer weather and potential "second milk peak" on milk volumes.
  • Evolution of global dairy demand, particularly China's import appetite.
  • Resolution or persistence of customs disputes and their impact on exports.
  • Continued monitoring of input costs (feed, energy) and new tariff impacts.
  • Overall balance of production and demand, especially as holiday season concludes.
